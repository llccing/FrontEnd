The key reasons are:

Using an Auto Scaling group ensures the EC2 instances that process orders are highly available and scalable. With SQS, the orders are decoupled from the instances that process them via asynchronous queuing. If instances fail or go down, the orders remain in the queue until new instances can pick them up.
